## What to avoid when selecting a logistics software development company

### 1. Companies without proven logistics experience
Avoid vendors who claim general software development expertise but lack specific logistics portfolio examples. Building logistics software requires understanding fleet constraints, dispatch rules, delivery windows, warehouse workflows, and regulatory compliance. 

**Red flags:**
- No case studies from logistics, transportation, or supply chain clients
- Generic portfolio showing only standard web/mobile apps
- Inability to discuss logistics-specific challenges during discovery calls
- No references from freight forwarders, 3PLs, carriers, or warehouse operators

### 2. One-size-fits-all solutions
Logistics operations vary dramatically between industries and business models. A last-mile delivery platform differs fundamentally from freight forwarding systems or warehouse automation. Companies pushing off-the-shelf products without customization capabilities cannot address your unique workflows, integration requirements, or scalability needs.

**Red flags:**
- Reluctance to discuss custom feature development
- Rigid pricing models with limited flexibility
- No discussion of API integration with existing systems
- Template-based approaches without workflow analysis

### 3. No post-launch support strategy
Logistics software requires ongoing maintenance, feature updates, bug fixes, and scalability adjustments. Companies disappearing after launch leave you with technical debt and no path forward for growth or system improvements.

**Red flags:**
- Vague or missing support terms in contracts
- No dedicated support team mentioned
- Reluctance to discuss SLAs or response times
- Focus only on development without mentioning ongoing maintenance

### 4. Lack of real-time data expertise
Logistics depends on real-time tracking, live route updates, instant notifications, and synchronized data across multiple platforms. Companies without expertise in WebSockets, MQTT, real-time databases, or streaming data protocols cannot build the responsive systems modern logistics requires.

**Red flags:**
- No mention of real-time capabilities in portfolio
- Unfamiliarity with real-time communication protocols
- Batch processing approaches for time-sensitive operations
- No experience with GPS tracking or live mapping
